Stress Management Techniques

Stress management offers a range of strategies to help you better deal with stress and difficulty (adversity) in your life.

Anxiety Disorder Symptoms

1. Excessive worry

2. Sleeping disorders

3. Overwhelming and irrational fear

4. Clenching your jaw, balling your fists and constant muscle tension

5. Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)

Emotional symptoms of anxiety disorders

1. Feeling apprehensive and dreadful

2. Trouble focusing and concentrating

3. Expecting the worst

4. Feeling irritated often

5. Experiencing restlessness

Physical symptoms of anxiety disorders

1. Sweating

2. Higher heart rate

3. Fatigue

4. Insomnia

5. Hot and cold flushes

6. Increase frequency of bathroom usage

Tips for managing anxiety and stress

1. Exercise regularly

  • Physical exercise can be an excellent way to reduce stress and anxiety. It helps to release endorphins, which are natural mood-enhancing chemicals that can improve your overall sense of well-being.

    • 2. Practice relaxation techniques

  • Relaxation techniques such as deep breathing, meditation, and yoga can be effective for reducing stress and anxiety. These practices can help you to calm your mind and body and reduce the physical symptoms of stress.

    • 3. Prioritize self-care

  • Taking care of yourself is essential for managing stress and anxiety. Make sure that you are getting enough sleep, eating a healthy diet, and engaging in activities that you enjoy.

    • 4. Stay connected with others

  • Social support is crucial for managing stress and anxiety. Make sure that you are staying connected with friends and family, and don't hesitate to reach out for help when you need it.

    • 5. Manage your time effectively

  • Poor time management can contribute to stress and anxiety. Try to create a schedule that allows you to prioritize your most important tasks and avoid overloading yourself with too many responsibilities.
